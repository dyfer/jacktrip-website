Understanding latency and why it is important

All communications create latency as it takes time for a sound to travel across distance. When making a sound, the further away you are from someone, the longer it takes to be heard.

What is JackTrip?
It is…

JackTrip Open Source Project

JackTrip is a software application that enables the live performance of music over the Internet by dramatically reducing the audio latency common in other online collaborations solutions. It was developed at Stanford University by Professor Chris Chafe’s team in 2000-2007. It was published under an MIT open source license in 2007 (copyrighted by Juan-Pablo Caceres and Chris Chafe at Stanford University) and has since built a large community of many advocates and over a dozen contributors.

JackTrip Foundation

The Foundation will conduct charitable and educational activities based on the JackTrip Open Source Platform. Specifically, the Foundation plans to further musical arts and education by providing JackTrip Open Source Platform products and services to nonprofit musical arts organizations.

In addition, the Foundation also plans to conduct educational programs for technological and musical users of the JackTrip Open Source Platform. We plan to produce seminars, educational videos, articles, and workshops addressing a wide range of topics regarding the intersection of music and computer technology.

Virtual Studio

In an effort to make JackTrip easier to use for a broad audience, we developed a web application that automates the configuration, deployment and management of the JackTrip software over the Internet. We also built plug-and-play boxes using Raspberry Pi computers with custom images that enable them to be fully managed via the same web application. This hardware and software combination makes it easy for groups of any size to quickly get up and running with JackTrip without requiring deep technology skills.
